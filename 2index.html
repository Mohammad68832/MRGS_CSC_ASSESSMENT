What is Kaitiakitanga & Manaakitanga?

Kaitiakitanga is a very important Maori concept that often means a lot to the people and their culture. It is a concept that encompasses all aspects of nature, including the lands, skies, and seas, and highlights how important they are to the people and how they can be conserved with the right mindset. Kaitiakitanga is a Maori word that means guardianship and protection, and is often used to link to the protection and conservation of native flora and fauna. Kaitiakitanga is not just about managing resources, but also maintaining a balance between the people and nature. The Māori people were quite advanced in their methods and understood nature very well, being able to use certain parts of nature when they needed it, and banning the usage of the area for material for a while for it to recover. In conclusion, kaitiakitanga is an old Maori practice that concerns the guardianship and protection of the land and sea that surrounds it.


Manaakitanga is another very important Maori concept that often relates to respect, responsibility and care in our modern society. However, if taken seriously and applied to every aspect of life, it could also be applied to things like nature. If we were to respect our nature, it would be easier to incorporate practises like Kaitiakitanga, and would make it easier to protect, care, and preserve our environment for future generations. Similar goes for responsibility, if we were all to take responsibility for our own actions and for our environment, it would be much easier for us and environmental workers to apply kaitiakitanga into our environments. And finally, if we were actually to care about nature and our exotic plant and wildlife, we wouldn’t even need to take all the precautionary actions that are being applied currently. This means that together, along with kaitiakitanga, if carried by every person in their hearts, can destroy pollution and any other things that are currently helping to destroy our environments.
